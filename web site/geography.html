China’s Vast and Diverse Landscape

Covering 9.6 million square kilometers, China is the fourth largest country in the world. Its geography is incredibly diverse, ranging from towering mountains to fertile plains and expansive deserts.

Major Geographic Features

  • Mountains: Himalayas (including Mount Everest), Kunlun, and Tianshan ranges.
  • Rivers: Yangtze River (longest in Asia), Yellow River, Pearl River.
  • Deserts: Gobi Desert and Taklamakan Desert.
  • Plains: North China Plain, a key agricultural region.
  • Coastline: Over 14,000 km along the Pacific Ocean.

China’s geography has played a vital role in shaping its history, economy, and culture, providing natural barriers, fertile land, and access to trade routes.
