China’s Historical Background

China is one of the world’s oldest civilizations, with a recorded history spanning thousands of years. From the early dynasties such as the Shang and Zhou, to the unification under the Qin Dynasty in 221 BCE, China has developed a rich cultural and political heritage.

Key Historical Periods

  • Ancient Dynasties: Shang, Zhou, Qin, Han
  • Medieval China: Tang and Song Dynasties — known for art, science, and trade
  • Ming Dynasty: Famous for exploration and the construction of much of the Great Wall
  • Qing Dynasty: The last imperial dynasty, ending in 1911
  • Modern Era: Establishment of the People’s Republic of China in 1949

China’s history is marked by innovation, resilience, and transformation, shaping not only its own society but also influencing the world through trade, philosophy, and culture.
